Objective
This logbook documents the design and construction of a custom-built wheeled mobile robot (WMR) developed for low-cost indoor boundary mapping. The platform utilizes dual optical flow odometry, a MEMS gyroscope, and ultrasonic ranging on a single onboard controller, enabling high-frequency dead reckoning and wall-following navigation under strict cost and compute constraints.

Mechanical Assembly
  • •Locomotion: two DC gear motors mounted on a rigid base plate in a differential-drive configuration. Forward motion and steering are achieved through differential speed control.
  • •Optical flow unit: OFS1 and OFS2 are fixed to a shared acrylic plate on the chassis underside in near-constant ground contact. Reference points are separated by a caliper-measured baseline d = 99.51 mm, with the sensing x-axis aligned to the robot's forward direction.
  • •Perception sensors: The ISM330DHCX IMU is rigidly mounted on the chassis. HC-SR04 units face the front, rear, and lateral sides for wall following and boundary-point extraction.
4
Electrical and Wiring
  • •Actuation: Raspberry Pi 5 GPIO supplies PWM and direction signals to the L298N dual H-bridge, whose outputs drive the two DC motors.
  • •Power: The 11.1 V Li-ion battery powers the motor supply via an integrated switch, with a regulated 5 V rail powering the sensors, while a power bank supplies power to the Raspberry Pi 5.
  • •Sensor interfaces: OFS1/OFS2 via USB, the ISM330DHCX via I²C, and the HC-SR04 trigger/echo lines via GPIO.
5
Software, Data Logging, and Calibration
  • •Fusion and logging: the Pi 5 polls all sensors and logs timestamped data. Optical-flow displacement and gyroscopic yaw rate are fused at 100 Hz (Δt = 0.01 s). Poses and projected boundary points are stored for offline trajectory reconstruction.
  • •Per-trial calibration: OFS scale factors are obtained as s = C_avg / d_s by translating the sensors on a uniform section of the test surface. Two fixed end points (mechanical stoppers) were positioned at a precisely measured distance (d_s) apart. The sensors were repeatedly moved between these stoppers, ensuring that each traversal covered the same known distance along a reference flat vertical surface. Multiple trials were conducted, and the corresponding sensor counts were recorded to determine the average number (C_avg) of units measured.
  • •The gyroscope bias b is estimated under stationary conditions before each run, and the OFS baseline d is measured using callipers.
